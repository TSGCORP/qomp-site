§ Product

Three sources of truth.

Qomp combines your HRIS data, Salary.com market intelligence, and TSG advisory expertise into a single dashboard. Connected once, updated continuously, interpreted quarterly. Below: how it actually works.

§ 01 — The data architecture

What goes in. What comes out.

Most comp tools force you to choose: free crowdsourced data with no advisor, or a $30K enterprise engagement. Qomp connects three streams that traditionally lived in different vendor relationships — and unifies them in one dashboard.

Source 01 · Internal
Your HRIS, connected.
A live API connection to BambooHR, Rippling, or ADP. Your full employee roster — names, titles, locations, base salaries, hire dates — syncs nightly without your team touching a spreadsheet.
Nightly sync · No CSV uploads
Source 02 · Market
Salary.com CAMD.
The same dataset Fortune 500 comp teams use. 8,712 companies, 247K+ benchmarked roles, refreshed monthly. HR-reported and validated — not crowdsourced from anonymous employees with an axe to grind.
Monthly refresh · City-level precision
Source 03 · Advisory
TSG, interpreting.
Every Qomp plan includes a TSG compensation advisor. Quarterly written reports. Direct access for ad-hoc questions. The interpretation layer that data alone cannot provide.
Quarterly review · Direct access

§ 02 — Six modules

Built around the questions HR teams actually ask.

Six dashboard modules, each designed around a real conversation that happens in HR offices every week. No filler. No vanity metrics. Each module exists because clients asked us to build it.

Module 01
Pay positioning overview.
A single-screen view of where every role sits against market — P10 through P90, with at-risk roles flagged automatically. The first thing you see when you log in.
Answers
  • Who's paid below market right now?
  • Which divisions need attention this quarter?
  • How does our average compa-ratio compare?
Module 02
Role explorer.
Search, filter, and drill into every individual position. See market range, current pay, percentile placement, and proposed adjustment — for every employee.
Answers
  • Where exactly does Sarah's base land vs market?
  • What would it cost to bring her to P50?
  • Who else holds her title across the company?
Module 03
Geographic analysis.
Pay positioning by metro area and country. Critical for hybrid companies and multi-location employers — Phoenix doesn't pay Boise rates, and your data should reflect that.
Answers
  • Are our remote hires paid correctly for their cities?
  • Which offices have the most pay risk?
  • What's our international compa by country?
Module 04
Pay equity diagnostics.
Comp ratios sliced by division, function, and tenure. Identifies gaps that need attention — without making demographic claims you don't have data to support.
Answers
  • Where are our largest internal pay gaps?
  • Are there functions systematically underpaid?
  • What would closing the gaps cost?
Module 05
Flight risk modeling.
Combines compa-ratio, tenure, and role criticality to surface the employees most likely to receive competitive offers. Not a guess — a triangulated estimate based on three data points.
Answers
  • Who's most at risk of leaving for pay?
  • What's the cost of a retention adjustment?
  • Who deserves a pre-emptive raise?
Module 06
TSG insights.
The quarterly written interpretation from TSG's advisors. Findings, prioritized actions, and recommendations specific to your dataset. Numbers are easy. Decisions are hard.
Answers
  • What does this data actually mean for us?
  • What should we do before merit cycle?
  • What would an outside expert recommend?
