Dashboard modules

Six views. Every comp question answered.

Each module in Qomp answers a specific question your leadership team is already asking.

Pay positioning
"Where does our pay sit vs. the market?" See every department's alignment to Salary.com benchmarks at a glance. Spot the departments that are at risk before people start leaving.
Compa-ratio analysis
"Who's underpaid vs. the market midpoint?" Every employee's compa-ratio calculated and ranked. At-risk roles flagged automatically when they drop below the 85th percentile.
Pay band management
"Are our salary ranges still competitive?" Build and manage salary bands tied to live market data. Get alerted when a hire or promotion pushes someone outside their range.
Merit cycle planning
"How do we allocate this year's merit budget?" Manager workflows, budget pools, and approval chains built in. Approved changes write back to your HRIS automatically.
Pay equity analysis
"Do we have unexplained pay gaps?" Statistical gap analysis by gender, tenure, and department. Remediation modeling and board-ready audit reports included.
TSG quarterly report
"What should we actually do about all this?" Written interpretation from TSG's advisors every quarter. Specific recommendations tied to your data, not generic best practices.

About TSG & Qomp

Comp expertise, finally in a product.

I'm Stuart Reeves, founder of TSG Executive Search & Compensation. For over a decade, I've been helping mid-size employers attract, retain, and fairly compensate their people — through executive search, HR consulting, and compensation strategy.

Every engagement taught me the same lesson: most mid-size employers are making compensation decisions in the dark. They can't afford Mercer or Radford. They don't have a dedicated comp analyst. And self-serve tools like CompAnalyst require expertise they don't have in-house.

Qomp is the product I always wished existed — Salary.com's trusted market data, wrapped in a dashboard built for non-specialists, with TSG's advisory layer on top so clients get answers, not just numbers.

This is not a venture-backed startup. It's a product built by an HR practitioner for HR practitioners — funded by real client relationships and a decade of comp consulting experience.

What we believe

Four principles behind every Qomp decision.

01
Data without interpretation is just noise.
Every Qomp plan includes a TSG quarterly insight report because market data without context isn't useful. Knowing you're 15% below market means nothing without understanding which roles to prioritize and what to do about it.
02
Mid-market employers deserve enterprise-grade comp tools.
Mercer and Radford are built for companies with 5,000+ employees and dedicated comp teams. The 400-person healthcare company has the same pay decisions to make — and no tools to make them with confidence.
03
Trusted data is the only data worth building on.
Qomp runs on Salary.com's CAMD dataset — 100% HR-reported, validated compensation data from 8,700+ companies. We chose it because we've used it for 10 years and trust it. Crowdsourced data doesn't belong in a comp decision.
04
Fair pay starts with visibility.
You can't fix pay gaps you can't see. Qomp makes the invisible visible — who's underpaid, which roles are at risk, and what it costs to close the gap. Equity isn't a value statement. It's a data problem that needs a data solution.
